## Highlights
Levitin explains that our brains have limited attention and memory capacity, so we must “move the burden of organizing from our brain to the external world” to conserve mental energy ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jvac774whjmnjg3zv4yjf7bg))

The book (and Forte’s blog) emphasize that our biological memory can only juggle a few items at once, so an external system serves as an extension of the mind. By offloading information to a trusted external repository, we reduce overwhelm and make space for creativity. Forte describes a Second Brain as an **external, centralized digital repository** of personal knowledge, built so that it “expands our memory and our intellect using modern tools” ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jvacnwnqfvmj5m26w9cpgnn7))

Key cognitive insights in this book include the importance of *elaboration* (writing notes in your own words to process ideas deeply) and *making connections* between notes to mirror the associative nature of memory. One takeaway is that writing things down relieves the brain of the need to memorize, allowing it to focus on thinking. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jvac6nyxwknv5x3v97sbm3kx))
