## Summary
Using AI tools like ChatGPT can be a valuable aid in the writing process. While there may be skepticism and misunderstanding surrounding AI writing, it can actually enhance the work of experienced and talented writers. AI can help capture thoughts, organize ideas, summarize complex concepts, overcome writer's block, and evaluate writing. Incorporating AI into the writing practice is a personal choice, and there is no one-size-fits-all approach. However, AI has the potential to be a powerful creative tool that enables writers to produce their best work.

## Highlights
How to incorporate AI into your writing practice
If we map the writing process out from start to finish, there are a few obvious places where AI can be effectively incorporated. It can help you:
• **Get** your thoughts down when you want to
• **Organize** your thoughts before you get started on a piece
• **Capture** a voice when you want the flavor of a particular writer
• **Summarize** complex ideas when you’re trying to explain
• **Help** you when you get stuck
• **Evaluate** your writing when you need a fresh brain
Before we start, though, let’s get a few things straight. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01hbmk9rvdtnt2tp8zrhzg1j70))

What I’ve been doing recently is taking a walk and recording myself free-associating while I’m out. I say anything that comes to mind—good, bad, embarrassing, or otherwise. I feel like a loon while I’m doing it, but I gather a lot of raw material this way.
Then I have the AI transcribe the recording and summarize it into bullet points. The bullet points give me a list of interesting thoughts and ideas I’ve had while I’m out—and often the best ones turn into ideas for a piece.
