## Highlights
Founders and investors in these startups project demand may pick up if more companies, from retailers to healthcare companies, develop their own AI rather than buying it from firms such as **OpenAI** and **Anthropic**. The recent rise of open-source AI models such **DeepSeek** has boosted corporate interest in developing models in-house, which could spur more business for the model-evaluation firms.
“As the market grows, [evaluation software will] get adopted by less sophisticated customers,” said an investor in one of the startups. “We’re still in the earliest phases.” ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jykm6wasmng1ctskx8q3by76))

Still, such corporate customers tend to take more time before signing AI contracts than individual developers, in part because their contracts are bigger. This may be why evaluation startups are showing slower revenue growth compared to [other AI startups](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/startups-ai-revenue-is-booming-some-investors-doubt-it-will-last), such as **Cursor**-maker **Anysphere** and **StackBlitz**.
Four-year-old **Galileo**, which develops tools to test the performance of generative AI systems, targets customers including Fortune 100 companies. Initial contracts with customers typically start out between $100,000 and $250,000, said a person close to the company. The company was generating at least $5 million in annualized revenue as of its fiscal year ending January, said the same person.
