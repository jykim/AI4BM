## Summary
Pacific Crest Trail hiking advice for 2025 and beyond as told by the PCT Class of 2024 - from hiking attitudes and resupply to trail culture.

## Highlights
Stop and smell the roses. Turn around—sometimes, the best views are behind you. Rush less and take more sidequests. In general, plan less and roll with the punches more. Don’t rush to get a [trail family](https://www.halfwayanywhere.com/trails/unpopular-opinion-why-your-trail-family-sucks/)—let it happen naturally. **Don’t be afraid to leave and hike alone—you’ll meet plenty of amazing people**. Take photos of people; the views can get repetitive even though they’re amazing. Contact trail angels. They want to help and love hearing your stories. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jq2vd8hsrner3gaqntrhabj0))

**So please, let the trail change you**. Let yourself be happy, let yourself cry and be mad and break down, let yourself feel all of you. Grieve for who you used to be but don’t hold onto what you were just because it’s comfortable, you can climb mountains and cross deserts, you can do this too, you’re strong enough to get out here, now be strong enough to commit to yourself, not just these miles ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jq2ve4zfwb1e5ee0cdenxb6f))

• **Spend less time researching and more time hiking and training**! The fitter you are when starting, the more you will enjoy the desert – which is mentally tough, especially in the first few weeks. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jq3037vdpptnsf7k6kcxtr0q))

• When you meet someone on the trail, you never know if you will never see them again or if you will hike with them every day for the next five months. **Treat everyone as if it’s the latter**. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jq30504fz3t99tz3zxtyr27x))

• I can’t say it enough: **don’t quit on a bad day**. You don’t want to regret that decision.
• **It’s worth pushing through the suck**. Any time you think you can’t do something, know that you can. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01jq3076fmrg3zdarvampantrh))
